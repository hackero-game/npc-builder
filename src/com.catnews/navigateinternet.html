Navigate the internet

Navigating the Internet can sometimes be really difficult. We have asked trustworthy seniors about their biggest problems while navigating the world wide web and this is what they answered:

Lost Webpages

Kathleen Montes (65) loves looking for new recipes on the internet. Her son helped her setup a modem in her house and now she is online 4 hours a day searching for the perfect apple pie recipe. However one of her biggest pains is that when she finds a great new recipe but forgets to print the page. "I'm missing great recipes and feel really guilty that I could not share them with my friend Maria James" she said. Well Kathleen no more worries, we got the perfect solution for you. Most browser nowaday have a bookmark feature to help you remember the best recipes of all time. But not just that, browserdevelopers really worked hard to extend this feature for even more webpages. Found an epic cat meme? Bookmark it. Found the cheapest fridge on amadon.com? Well bookmarks got you covered! Googled for the Hindi word for murder a bookmark can save your day!

Find new webpages

Mark Wise (78) recently got a modem and now explores the world wide web on his own. He already found many pages he likes, but the ones with bears are his favourites. "I always feel happy after visiting these pages. bears are just so relaxing" he explained. However Mark is pretty sure there must be more on the Internet than his favourite bear page so he asked our reported for some hints on how to find new webpages.

The great answer is YES! there are ways. Some websites keep small references to other pages. They can be hidden in the footer or on subpages. A great example is coffeegreen.com which references the website building tool they used to create their page. But not just that! Adds sometimes can also be a great reference to find other webpages. so keep you eyes out for these small little helpers. and also everytime you click on a add you make a bird wearing a suit really happy by making dollar signs appear in its eyes. So keep your eyes open for small references, adds and other hints. This search for hints makes your online experience really amazing!
